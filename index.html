About

I am a Research Fellow in the Faculty of Environment and Information Studies at Keio University, Japan. I received my Ph.D. in Robotics Engineering from the University of Technology Sydney (UTS), Australia, in 2023.

Prior to joining Keio University, I worked as a Postdoctoral Research Associate at UTS and as a Project Researcher at The University of Tokyo, where I conducted research on continuum robotics, soft robotics, and intelligent robotic systems.

My research lies at the intersection of continuum robotics, soft robotics, variable stiffness mechanisms, tactile sensing, and embodied intelligence. I am particularly interested in developing perception-driven robotic systems capable of safe and adaptive interaction with humans and unstructured environments.

Research Interests

My research focuses on continuum and soft robots with variable stiffness, multimodal tactile sensing, perception-driven control, and embodied intelligence. I aim to develop robots that can safely interact with humans and adapt to complex environments.
